## What Is Unchained Music?

Unchained Music is a UK-based independent music distribution platform built specifically for indie artists and labels. The idea behind it is pretty refreshing — instead of acting as a middleman that skims your earnings forever, they charge a straightforward annual subscription and step out of the way of your money.

You upload your tracks, they distribute to Spotify, Apple Music, TikTok, YouTube, Tidal, Deezer, and 220+ other platforms globally. Every release goes through a manual human review (more on why that matters in a second), and royalties come back to you — all of them.

Artists like Daryl Hall, Dave Stewart, Markus Schulz, and Punk Rock Factory (810M+ streams) all distribute through Unchained. That's quite a roster for a platform that charges less per year than a single lunch out.

## The Human Review Thing — Actually a Big Deal

Most distributors these days are fully automated. You upload, a bot checks some boxes, it goes live. Fast, but also how you end up with ghost tracks, fraudulent streams, and metadata disasters.

Unchained does manual human review on every single release. Every one. 

One artist, Kristi Lowe (40M+ streams), mentioned that the human-led metadata check caught a miscredited remix before it went live — something that could have triggered a takedown after the fact. That kind of quality control is normally something only artists with proper label deals get access to. Here it's just... baked in.

The tradeoff is processing time (5 days on Grow, 2 on Pro), but if you're planning your release schedule in advance anyway, that's a non-issue. And if you need something out faster, there's an expedited option that gets it to the top of the queue within 1 business day.

## Beyond Basic Distribution

Unchained isn't just a distribution pipe. The platform has been building out a full suite of services that used to be label territory:

**Royalty Advances** — Apply to get your streaming income upfront. Useful if you need to fund a music video, a tour, or a marketing push before the streams actually come in. Ashley Wallbridge (33M+ streams) cited an advance as what made her first video and pre-save campaign possible.

**AI Mastering** — A built-in AI mastering tool to get your audio release-ready. It's a paid add-on but available to all plan tiers.

**Playlist Pitching** — Both third-party playlist pitching (available on Grow) and editorial playlist pitching to Spotify and Apple Music editorial teams (Pro only).

**DIY Digital Marketing** — Automated ad tools with a 20% management fee. You set the budget, they handle the campaign mechanics.

**Physical Distribution & Vinyl Pressing** — Yes, they press vinyl. If you want to sell physical records or distribute to brick-and-mortar stores, that's an option.

**Catalog Management** — For labels managing multiple artists and catalogs, there's a whole layer of tools for catalog admin, royalty reporting in 96+ currencies, and sync opportunities.

## What People Are Actually Saying

The honest picture from Trustpilot is mixed in the way most platforms are mixed — the majority of users are happy and quietly getting on with it; the visible negative reviews tend to involve edge cases (ISRC code confusion on previously-released tracks, frustration with the AI music policy, one support interaction that went sideways).

The platform's response to criticism seems reasonably transparent — they explain their policies, acknowledge where things could be clearer, and don't seem to be burying feedback.

For context, Punk Rock Factory (yes, the same band, 810M+ streams) specifically mentioned using the Pro plan to manage five artists with unified analytics. That's a real-world stress test of the multi-profile feature, and it held up.

## Who Is This Actually For?

**Grow ($14.99/year)** makes sense if you're a solo artist, releasing regularly, want 100% royalties, and don't need label-tier features. At that price, there's basically no reason not to have a proper distributor setup.

**Pro ($29.99/year)** is the sweet spot if you're managing multiple projects or sub-labels, need faster processing, want editorial pitching access, or are doing video content seriously. Two business days turnaround plus editorial access for $30/year is genuinely hard to beat.

**Core (Invite-Only)** — worth applying for if you already have meaningful streaming numbers and want to build a relationship with an independent platform that's selective about who it works with.
